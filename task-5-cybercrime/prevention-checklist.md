🛡️  STAY SAFE ONLINE
A Cybersecurity Checklist for College Students in India
Digital Literacy Programme  |  2026

Use this checklist to protect yourself from the most common online threats faced by students today. Tick each box as you apply it — your digital safety is in your hands.
🔐  Section 1: Account & Password Security

☐  Tip 1: Use Strong, Unique Passwords for Every Account
Create passwords with at least 12 characters, combining uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across college portals, email, and social media. Use a trusted password manager like Bitwarden or Google Password Manager.

☐  Tip 2: Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Turn on 2FA on all important accounts — Gmail, Instagram, banking apps, and college portals. This adds a second layer of security so even if your password is stolen, your account remains protected.

☐  Tip 3: Never Share OTPs, PINs, or Passwords with Anyone
No bank, government body, or tech company will ever ask for your OTP or password over call, WhatsApp, or SMS. If someone asks — it is a scam. Hang up immediately.

💳  Section 2: UPI & Financial Safety  ★ Priority

☐  Tip 4: 💳 Understand the Difference Between 'Send' and 'Collect' on UPI
Entering your UPI PIN on a collect request SENDS money — it does not receive it. If a stranger sends you a collect request claiming to transfer money to you, reject it immediately. Legitimate senders do not need your PIN.

☐  Tip 5: 💳 Never Scan QR Codes from Unverified Sources
Fraudsters on OLX, Facebook Marketplace, and WhatsApp send fake QR codes that debit your account the moment you scan them. Always verify the seller's identity independently before scanning any payment QR code.

🎣  Section 3: Phishing & Online Scams

☐  Tip 6: Think Before You Click — Verify Links Before Opening
Phishing links often mimic trusted sites (e.g., 'sbi-login.net' instead of 'sbi.co.in'). Hover over any link before clicking. When in doubt, go directly to the official website by typing the URL in your browser.

☐  Tip 7: Be Suspicious of 'Too Good to Be True' Offers
Fake scholarship offers, lottery wins, job offers requiring upfront fees, and free iPhone giveaways are common traps targeting college students. If you did not apply for it, it is almost certainly a scam.

📱  Section 4: Social Media & Privacy

☐  Tip 8: Audit Your Social Media Privacy Settings Regularly
Set Instagram, Snapchat, and Facebook profiles to private. Avoid publicly posting your phone number, college name, hostel address, or daily routine. Cybercriminals use this information for targeted phishing and stalking.

☐  Tip 9: Use Secure, Password-Protected Wi-Fi Only
Avoid using public Wi-Fi at cafes, bus stands, or railway stations for banking or logging into personal accounts. If you must, use a trusted VPN. Attackers on open networks can intercept your data using man-in-the-middle attacks.

☐  Tip 10: Keep Your Devices and Apps Updated
Software updates patch security vulnerabilities that hackers exploit. Enable automatic updates on your phone, laptop, and apps — especially browsers and banking applications. An outdated app is an open door for attackers.

Report as soon as possible — early reporting significantly increases the chance of freezing fraudulent transactions.
